Basic Pasta Dough Recipe

Here’s a simple recipe for a basic pasta dough:

Ingredients:
- 2 cups semolina flour or all-purpose flour
- 3 large eggs
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- Water, as needed

Instructions:
1. On a clean surface or in a mixing bowl, mound the flour and create a well in the center.
2. Crack the eggs into the well, add the salt, and use a fork to whisk the eggs gently, gradually incorporating the flour.
3. Once the mixture becomes too stiff to mix with a fork, use your hands to knead the dough until smooth and elastic, about 8-10 minutes.
4. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes at room temperature. This resting period allows the gluten to relax, making it easier to roll out.

Rolling and Shaping Pasta

Once the dough has rested, it’s time to roll it out and shape it into your favorite pasta forms. Here are some popular pasta shapes and how to create them:

4. Unfold the strips and let them dry on a floured surface.

Ravioli:

2. Place a teaspoon of filling (such as ricotta cheese or a meat mixture) in the center of each square.
3. Moisten the edges with a little water, fold the dough over, and press to seal, making sure to remove any air pockets.

Penne:
1. Roll out the dough and cut it into rectangles approximately 2 inches by 1 inch.
2. Roll each rectangle around a thin wooden dowel or the handle of a spoon to form a tube shape.
3. Cut the ends at an angle to create the classic penne shape.

Cooking Fresh Pasta

Fresh pasta cooks much faster than dried pasta, usually taking only 2-4 minutes to become al dente. To cook your pasta:
